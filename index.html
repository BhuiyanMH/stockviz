Market Response to the Covid-19 Crisis

Welcome to Stockviz!

Here, we will be exploring how the economy reacted to the government measures of Covid-19. We will try to assess the economic impact through the stock markets. How companies of different industries have responded to Covid also will be explored.
Let's start with the overall view of stock markets of Asia, America, and Europe. The Asian market index is Hang Seng, and it is consists of China, South Korea, Singapore, and Hong Kong. The name of the American index is S&P 500, and the European index is STOXX 50 EU.
The Confinement Index (CI) represents an overall view of the strictness of confinement measures. It is calculated using the same procedure of the confinement data based on only the confinement indicators. The higher value represents more strict confinement measures.

We can see a consistent drop for a while in the index of the Asian market as China implemented a lockdown in Wuhan from 23rd January 2020. Big drops are seen in the American and European markets when the governments of respective countries started to set strategies to implement lockdown that started from the 3rd week of February to different weeks of March in various states and provinces.

Now let's analyze the stability of the markets in detail. In the following candlesticks chart, the daily open and close values of an index are represented by candlesticks. The shadow of the candlesticks shows the high and low of a particular day. The vertical lines represent changes in the confinement rules. Hover on the line or candlesticks to get the details.
The green candlesticks represent that the close value of an index is higher than the open value whereas the red ones represent the opposite. Long green candlesticks indicate there is a strong buying pressure and typically implies that the price is bullish. Contrarily, long red candlesticks indicate that there is a significant selling pressure and are suggestive of the price to be bearish.

Now, we have a general overview of the response of markets to Covid confinement measures! Let's analyze how different industries and companies responded to Covid! Ok let's go.
